Australia grows lots of fruits and vegetables locally. "Seasonality" alludes to the times of year when harvesting a crop is at its peak. Picking food locally when it's in season usually means it'll be fresh, full of flavour, nutritious, and cheaper.

About the data

Seasonality of fruit and vegetables varies by climate and weather conditions. A particularly dry or wet season can affect the timing and availability of certain crops. This data is a guide only.
