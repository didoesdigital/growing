Australia grows lots of fruit and vegetables locally. "Seasonality" alludes to the times of year when harvesting a crop is at its peak. Picking food locally when it's in season usually means it'll be fresh, full of flavour, nutritious, and cheaper.

About the data

Seasonality of fruit and vegetables varies by climate and weather conditions. A particularly dry or wet season can affect the timing availability of certain crops. This data is a guide only. For now, it's examining the general availability of fruit and vegetables in Australia rather than by region.

I'm gathering data from a few different sources and will update this page as I learn.
