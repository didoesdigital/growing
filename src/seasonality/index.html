Growing

by Diana MacDonald

Australia grows lots of fruits and vegetables locally. "Seasonality" means the times of year when harvesting a crop is at its peak. Picking food locally when it's in season usually means it'll be fresh, full of flavour, nutritious, and cheaper.

Seasonality varies by climate and weather conditions. A particularly dry or wet season can affect the timing and availability of certain crops. This information is a guide only.
